Built an interactive Microsoft Excel dashboard to analyze the financial performance of an FMCG retail business. The project focuses on identifying high-performing products and categories, detecting low-margin products, and analyzing revenue and profit trends to support business decision-making.

## Business Problem

A mid-sized FMCG company wants to understand:

- Which products and categories generate the highest revenue and profit?
- Which products reduce overall profitability?
- How are revenue and profit changing over time?
- Which categories offer the best balance between growth and profitability?
- Where should management focus future investments and cost optimization efforts?

## Key Business Insights

### 1. Beverages generated the highest revenue but the lowest margin
- Revenue: **$3.74M**
- Margin: **11.15%**

Despite generating the most sales, Beverages delivered the weakest profitability, indicating opportunities for pricing and cost optimization.

### 2. Household products generated the highest profit
- Profit: **$832.22K**
- Margin: **22.81%**

Household products emerged as the strongest profit-driving category.

### 3. Personal Care achieved the highest profit margin
- Margin: **25.98%**
- Profit: **$776.98K**

Personal Care demonstrated strong pricing power and efficient cost management.

### 4. Some top-selling products are weakening profitability

| Product | Revenue |
|----------|---------:|
| Instant Coffee Gold | $1.17M |
| Laundry Detergent | $0.93M |
| Shampoo Repair | $0.69M |

However, **Instant Coffee Gold** maintained only **6.72% margin**, making it one of the lowest-margin products despite strong sales.

### 5. Revenue increased consistently from 2023 to 2025

| Year | Revenue |
|------|---------:|
| 2023 | $4.59M |
| 2024 | $4.84M |
| 2025 | $5.02M |

The business demonstrated stable year-over-year growth.

### 6. Year-end periods drive exceptional performance

- Revenue Growth (Dec 2025): **39.14%**
- Profit Growth (Dec 2025): **46.16%**

Holiday and year-end demand significantly boost business performance.

---

## Financial Storytelling

The company generated **$14.45M in revenue** and **$3.31M in profit** while maintaining an average profit margin of **19.87%**.

Although revenue steadily increased over the three-year period, profitability varied significantly across categories. Beverages drove the largest share of revenue but delivered the weakest margins, while Household and Personal Care emerged as the strongest contributors to profit.

The analysis also revealed that some top-selling products generated strong revenue but relatively low profits, highlighting the importance of evaluating profitability alongside sales performance.

Finally, both revenue and profit peaked during year-end periods, suggesting strong seasonal demand and opportunities for targeted inventory and marketing strategies.

---

## Recommendations

- Review pricing strategies for low-margin products.
- Investigate costs associated with Beverage products.
- Increase focus on Household and Personal Care categories.
- Monitor products with margins below 10%.
- Leverage historical trends for future sales forecasting.
